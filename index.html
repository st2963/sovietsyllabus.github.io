This project presents works in ground-breaking research, as for 1959. One year is chosen only for a preliminary demonstration of what the project will be. The goal is to create a dynamic map, both physical and abstract, in order to show networks and schools within Soviet institutions.

About the project

 
        key works in humanities and STEM      

           

       
 

The project features two points of entry into the data:

• from the perspective of knowledge production (the relations between writers within one institution and with foreign ones)

• from the perspective of a book and its context, provided inside the hover menu on the map.

 

The institute analysis is based on spatio-temporal homogeneity and it presupposes shared values and ideas between a collective. These ideas may not be clearly articulated by the authors themselves, but they reproduce those ideas as agents of their environments.

 

  
 

The map shows the most important works, generated through the analysis of university and institution libraries across the Eastern bloc. The large orange titles are imported ground-breaking knowledge. Violet small titles show local "stars," who had impact mostly within their city. The number after the title is the index of how many books are available in the databases of the selected universities and institutions. The list of works does not represent the whole picture without an expert's interpretations. Thus, this map serves as a starting point to answer the question: how many schools of thought were in the Soviet Union in 1959? The classification is based on two axes: author's location and author's relation to other authors on the map (as a translator, an editor, an introductory note writer).
